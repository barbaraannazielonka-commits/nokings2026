On Saturday, March 28, 2026, millions of Americans poured into the streets of cities, suburbs, small towns and rural communities across all 50 states for the third wave of "No Kings" protests — what organisers called the largest single-day nonviolent demonstration in American history.

The movement began in June 2025 as opposition to the policies of the second Trump administration. By March 2026, it had grown to address a range of urgent issues: aggressive immigration enforcement tactics — including the fatal shooting of US citizens Renée Good and Alex Pretti by federal agents in Minneapolis in January 2026 — and the US military's involvement in a war with Iran.

"The defining story of this Saturday's mobilisation is not just how many people are protesting, but where they are protesting."

— Leah Greenberg, co-founder of Indivisible, which started the "No Kings" movement

Two-thirds of attendees came from outside major urban centres — including conservative-leaning states like Idaho, Wyoming, Utah and Montana — signalling a broadening of the movement beyond traditional liberal strongholds. The flagship event was held in St. Paul, Minnesota, where Bruce Springsteen performed and politicians including Senator Bernie Sanders and Governor Tim Walz spoke.

Internationally, solidarity demonstrations took place in Rome, Paris, Berlin, London and Amsterdam, showing the global resonance of the movement's message about democracy and authoritarian governance.

Context

A Timeline of Events

January 2025

Trump's Second Term Begins

Donald Trump is inaugurated for a second term as US President. Rapid rollout of executive orders including major changes to immigration enforcement.

June 2025 — No Kings Day 1

First "No Kings" Protests

The first nationwide No Kings protest day coincides with Trump's 79th birthday and a military parade in Washington D.C. The movement is born.

October 2025 — No Kings Day 2

Second Wave of Protests

The second round draws millions across the country. Over 200,000 in Washington D.C. alone. Trump responds by posting an AI-generated mocking video.

December 2025

Operation Metro Surge in Minnesota

Over 3,000 federal immigration agents descend on the Twin Cities area of Minneapolis–St. Paul. Allegations of excessive force are widespread.

January 2026

Fatal Shootings & National Outrage

Federal agents fatally shoot US citizens Renée Good and Alex Pretti in separate incidents in Minneapolis. Massive local protests erupt. Dozens of lawsuits are filed.

February 2026

US–Iran War Begins

The US begins military operations in Iran (Operation Epic Fury), adding another major issue to the growing protest movement.

March 28, 2026 — No Kings Day 3

Historic Day of Action

8–9 million people participate in 3,300+ events across all 50 states and several continents — the largest single-day protest in American history.

Word Bank

Key Vocabulary

Click any word to reveal its definition. Learning these terms will help you discuss and write about the No Kings protests with confidence.

Protesttap to reveal
A public demonstration in which people gather to express strong objection to or support for a cause, policy, or event.
Democracytap to reveal
A system of government in which power is held by the people, exercised directly or through elected representatives.
Dissenttap to reveal
The expression of opinions that are at variance with those commonly or officially held. In a democracy, dissent is a protected right.
Authoritariantap to reveal
Favouring or enforcing strict obedience to authority at the expense of personal freedom. Protesters use this word to describe leadership they oppose.
Civil Rightstap to reveal
The rights of citizens to political and social freedom and equality, such as freedom of speech, assembly, and protection from unlawful treatment.
ICEtap to reveal
Immigration and Customs Enforcement — a US federal agency responsible for enforcing immigration laws. Its aggressive operations sparked much of the No Kings movement.
Mobilisationtap to reveal
The process of organising people to take collective action, especially in a political or social movement.
Executive Ordertap to reveal
A directive issued by the US President with the force of law, without requiring approval from Congress. Protesters opposed several such orders.
Rhetorictap to reveal
The art of using language effectively and persuasively. Protesters and politicians both use rhetoric — understanding it helps you think critically about speeches and slogans.
Solidaritytap to reveal
Unity and agreement of feeling or action among individuals with a common interest. International solidarity protests in Europe showed support for the US movement.
Slogantap to reveal
A short, memorable phrase used in political campaigning or advertising. "No Kings" itself is a powerful slogan — brief, bold, and historically rooted.
Amendmenttap to reveal
A change or addition to a legal document. The First Amendment to the US Constitution protects freedom of speech, press, and peaceful assembly — rights central to the No Kings protests.
